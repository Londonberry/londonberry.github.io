# Tire-Kicker — Priority 3 (Tertiary)

> The browser who isn't buying today but might remember you tomorrow

**Document:** Trigger Map - Tertiary Persona
**Created:** 2026-04-06
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

## Who They Are

The Tire-Kicker is anyone who clicks "Start a conversation" without a concrete project or buying intent. They might be a competitor doing research, a student exploring the space, a future founder whose idea isn't ready yet, or someone who's just curious about the tech. They're browsing, not committing. They represent the majority of website visitors.

---

## Psychological Profile

The Tire-Kicker's dominant trait is **low-stakes curiosity**. They have no pressure to decide, no deadline, no budget earmarked. This makes them the hardest to convert in the short term — but they're not worthless. A Tire-Kicker today can become a Nervous First-Timer in 6 months or refer an Enterprise PM next week.

They value **entertainment and insight over utility**. They're not looking to buy — they're looking to learn, be impressed, or be entertained. The bar analogy from the brainstorming session is perfect: this is Ben at a conference happy hour. Fun, casual, generous with ideas, but not giving away the whole playbook.

Their relationship to the problem is **hypothetical or distant**. They might have a vague idea, or they might have no idea at all. They might just want to see what a conversational AI on a consultancy website feels like. Their engagement level is the lowest of the three archetypes, but their potential for long-term brand impression is significant.

---

## Internal State

When the Tire-Kicker starts a conversation, they feel **casual and uncommitted**. There's no anxiety, no urgency. They're browsing. They might be mildly curious, mildly bored, or mildly competitive (checking out what other companies are doing). The emotional register is **low-intensity amusement** — they'll engage as long as it's interesting and bounce the moment it feels like work or a sales pitch.

---

## Usage Context

**Access/Discovery:** Could arrive from anywhere — social media, search, a friend's recommendation, or just clicking around. Likely on mobile. Time of day varies. Attention span is short.

**Emotional State:** Casual, uncommitted, easily distracted. Low conversion urgency but high impression-formation potential.

**Behavior Pattern:** Short responses. Tests boundaries. Might ask weird or off-topic questions. Will disengage quickly if bored. Responds well to humor, surprises, and unexpected depth.

**Decision Criteria:**
- Is this interesting? (novelty signal)
- Am I learning something? (value signal)
- Is this worth telling someone about? (share signal)
- Am I being sold to? (exit signal if yes)

**Success Outcome:** Has an unexpectedly good conversation. Walks away with one memorable insight or moment. Tells one person about the experience. Returns in the future when they have a real need.

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers (Wants)

**✅ Be surprised or entertained — this should be better than a typical website chatbot**
- WHAT: A novel, engaging experience that breaks expectations
- WHY: Low-intent visitors need a reason to keep talking
- WHEN: From the very first message
- **Widget Promise:** Conference-booth energy. Humor, personality, unexpected questions. "What brings you here? Do you come here often?"

**✅ Learn something interesting without committing to anything**
- WHAT: A genuine insight about their space, their idea, or tech in general
- WHY: Value received without strings attached creates lasting positive impression
- WHEN: Mid-conversation, after initial engagement
- **Widget Promise:** One sharp, specific insight they haven't heard before. Plant the callback — something they'll think about later.

**✅ See what AI can actually do (curiosity about the tech itself)**
- WHAT: Experience a conversational AI that feels genuinely different
- WHY: Many visitors are drawn to the novelty of the technology
- WHEN: Throughout — the medium IS part of the message
- **Widget Promise:** The conversation itself demonstrates Londonberry's AI capability. The product IS the portfolio piece.

### Negative Drivers (Fears)

**❌ Fear of getting trapped in a sales funnel they didn't opt into**
- WHAT: The conversation turning into lead capture after seeming casual
- WHY: Bait-and-switch damages trust permanently
- WHEN: If conversation shifts tone from casual to qualifying
- **Widget Answer:** Depth gating — information access scales with engagement level. No forced conversion. Casual stays casual unless they choose to go deeper.

**❌ Fear of being boring or having "nothing to offer" this conversation**
- WHAT: Feeling like they need a real project to justify talking
- WHY: "Start a conversation" implies having something to say
- WHEN: At initiation, if the opening assumes project intent
- **Widget Answer:** Opening paths that don't require a project. "Just curious what you do? Cool, let me show you something interesting."

**❌ Discomfort with sharing information when they have no buying intent**
- WHAT: Being asked for details (name, email, company) when they're just browsing
- WHY: Feels disproportionate — they haven't decided this matters yet
- WHEN: If AI asks for contact info early
- **Widget Answer:** Zero information requirements for casual engagement. Identity is optional, conversation is free. Let depth emerge naturally.

## Transformation Journey

**Before:** "Let me see what this chatbot does. Probably another 'How can I help you today?' thing."

**During:** "Okay this is actually funny. And it just told me something really interesting about my industry. This company is different."

**After:** "I wasn't looking to hire anyone, but I had this wild conversation on a software company's website. When I'm ready to build something, I'm going back there."

---

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Generate Qualified Leads (PRIMARY):** Low direct conversion, but Tire-Kickers are the farm team. A memorable experience today = a qualified lead in 6 months. They also refer others.
- ✅ **Build Trust & Differentiation (SECONDARY):** The most likely persona to share the experience socially. "You have to try this website's AI chat." Word-of-mouth multiplier.
- ✅ **Operational Intelligence (TERTIARY):** Casual conversations reveal what the broader market finds interesting, confusing, or surprising about AI development services.
