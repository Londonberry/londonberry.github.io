# Nervous First-Timer — Priority 1 (Primary)

> The curious visitor with an idea they've never said out loud

**Document:** Trigger Map - Primary Persona
**Created:** 2026-04-06
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

## Who They Are

The Nervous First-Timer has an idea — maybe a product, maybe an app, maybe just a problem they wish someone would solve. They've been thinking about it for weeks or months. They found londonberry.com through search, a referral, or social media. They clicked "Start a conversation" because something about the site felt approachable. This is likely their first time talking to anyone about building custom software.

---

## Psychological Profile

The Nervous First-Timer's dominant trait is **cautious curiosity**. They're interested enough to click, but fragile enough to bounce at the first sign of corporate speak or aggressive qualification. They don't know what things cost, how long they take, or even if their idea is any good. This vulnerability is their defining characteristic.

They value **authenticity over polish**. They can smell a sales funnel from a mile away and will disengage the moment they feel like a "lead" rather than a person. What they want — though they may not know it yet — is someone to take their idea seriously. Not to validate it uncritically, but to engage with it genuinely. They want a collaborator, not a vendor.

Their relationship to the problem is **personal and emotional**. This isn't a job requirement or a business need — it's *their* idea. Sharing it feels vulnerable. They're worried about looking naive, being judged, or discovering their idea is worthless. The act of articulating it to another person (even an AI acting as Ben) is psychologically significant.

---

## Internal State

When the Nervous First-Timer starts a conversation, they feel a mixture of **excitement and anxiety**. The excitement comes from finally telling someone. The anxiety comes from not knowing what to expect — Will this be a sales pitch? Will they ask for my email immediately? Will they make me feel stupid? They're in what behavioral economists call a "hot state" — emotionally engaged, problem-aware, and willing to act, but only if the environment feels safe. One wrong move and the **fight-or-flight response kicks in** — they close the tab and probably don't come back.

---

## Usage Context

**Access/Discovery:** Found londonberry.com via Google search ("custom app development"), social media, or referral. Browsed the site, got a feel for the vibe, then clicked "Start a conversation." Likely on desktop. Possibly evening or weekend — this is personal time, not work.

**Emotional State:** Cautious optimism layered over vulnerability. High engagement potential if safety is established in first 30 seconds.

**Behavior Pattern:** Reads carefully. Watches for signals of authenticity. Tests the water with vague descriptions before committing to detail. Will share more if they feel heard. Will retreat if they feel processed.

**Decision Criteria:**
- Does this feel like talking to a real person? (authenticity signal)
- Am I getting something or just giving information? (value signal)
- Is it safe to share my idea? (trust signal)
- Is this going to cost more than I can afford? (budget anxiety)

**Success Outcome:** Shares their idea in enough detail that the AI can reflect it back ("so you're building something like X but with Y"), receives a prototype concept image of their idea, and feels excited rather than exposed. Willing to continue the conversation toward timeline and budget.

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers (Wants)

**✅ Have my idea taken seriously and reflected back with understanding**
- WHAT: Genuine engagement with their concept
- WHY: They've never told anyone — validation that it makes sense is powerful
- WHEN: During idea exploration phase of conversation
- **Widget Promise:** AI actively listens, asks clarifying questions, then synthesizes: "So you're thinking about X that does Y for Z people"

**✅ See my idea visualized — proof it could be real**
- WHAT: Tangible artifact (prototype image) of their concept
- WHY: Transforms abstract idea into something concrete they can point to
- WHEN: After idea articulation, before budget conversation
- **Widget Promise:** AI-generated concept image from conversation summary — the "wow moment"

**✅ Understand what this would actually cost without feeling judged for my budget**
- WHAT: Honest, shame-free pricing guidance
- WHY: Budget is the scariest unknown — they assume custom software is out of reach
- WHEN: After value delivery, when trust is established
- **Widget Promise:** Ben volunteers a number first ("in my experience, about $10K for a high-value first round"), then asks where they are — no shame either way

### Negative Drivers (Fears)

**❌ Fear of being sold to before being heard**
- WHAT: Encountering a lead-capture form disguised as a conversation
- WHY: Past experience with chatbots that ask for email in the first message
- WHEN: First 30 seconds of interaction
- **Widget Answer:** No email ask until value is delivered. Earn-before-you-ask rhythm.

**❌ Fear of looking naive or having a dumb idea**
- WHAT: Being judged, dismissed, or given a condescending response
- WHY: This idea is personal — rejection of the idea feels like rejection of them
- WHEN: When first describing their concept
- **Widget Answer:** Every idea gets genuine engagement. AI responds with curiosity, not evaluation. "That's interesting — tell me more about the Y part."

**❌ Fear of wasting time on something they can't afford**
- WHAT: Going through a long conversation only to discover the price is 10x their budget
- WHY: Time is precious, and the embarrassment of pricing themselves out feels bad
- WHEN: Throughout, but peaks during budget discussion
- **Widget Answer:** Pricing anchored early and honestly. "Still spitballing" is a perfectly valid answer.

## Transformation Journey

**Before:** "I have this idea but I don't know who to talk to, what it would cost, or if it's even any good."

**During:** "Wait — this AI actually gets what I'm describing. And it just showed me a concept image of my idea. That's... actually what I was thinking."

**After:** "I just had the most interesting conversation with a software company's website. They showed me what my idea could look like before asking me for anything. I want to talk to this Ben guy."

---

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Generate Qualified Leads (PRIMARY):** This is the persona most likely to convert from stranger to client. Their emotional journey through the conversation IS the qualification process. Warm response to vulnerability = real prospect.
- ✅ **Build Trust & Differentiation (SECONDARY):** The prototype image moment creates a story they tell others. "You have to try this — I told it my app idea and it showed me a mockup."
- ✅ **Operational Intelligence (TERTIARY):** Their unfiltered idea descriptions reveal what the market wants to build. Pure signal.
