# Enterprise PM — Priority 2 (Secondary)

> The decision-maker evaluating vendors who needs proof of speed and capability

**Document:** Trigger Map - Secondary Persona
**Created:** 2026-04-06
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

## Who They Are

The Enterprise PM is a product manager, director of innovation, or VP of engineering at a mid-to-large company. They have budget authority (or influence over it) and are actively evaluating vendors for a specific project. They found Londonberry through a referral, LinkedIn, or targeted search. They're comparing 3-5 shops and have limited time. Every interaction is an evaluation.

---

## Psychological Profile

The Enterprise PM's dominant trait is **efficient evaluation**. They don't browse — they assess. Every second on the site is either confirming or disconfirming Londonberry as a viable partner. They've been through vendor selection before and have a mental checklist: relevant experience, technical capability, communication quality, speed to engage.

They value **competence and directness** over warmth. While they appreciate authenticity (they're human), their decision-making is more analytical than emotional. They're looking for signals that this team can execute — not just ideate. Past work, speed of understanding, and ability to anticipate their needs matter more than charm.

Their relationship to the problem is **professional and time-pressured**. This isn't their idea — it's a project with stakeholders, timelines, and budget constraints. They need a partner who understands enterprise context: NDAs, sprint cadences, stakeholder management, and the difference between a prototype and a production system.

---

## Internal State

When the Enterprise PM starts a conversation, they feel **skeptical but hopeful**. They've talked to several vendors already — some were too salesy, some too junior, some couldn't articulate their process. They're **measuring** Londonberry from the first response. Does the AI understand what they're asking? Does it anticipate follow-up questions? Can it demonstrate relevant experience without being asked? The emotional register is **professional impatience** — they'll give it 60 seconds to prove it's worth continuing.

---

## Usage Context

**Access/Discovery:** Found via referral, LinkedIn, or search for "AI development consultancy" / "rapid prototyping agency." On desktop during work hours. Likely has a meeting in 30 minutes.

**Emotional State:** Evaluative, time-pressured, professionally skeptical. High conversion potential if competence is demonstrated fast.

**Behavior Pattern:** Direct questions. Expects concise answers. Will test knowledge with specifics ("have you worked with real-time data pipelines?"). Skips pleasantries. Values substance over style.

**Decision Criteria:**
- Can they do what I need? (capability signal)
- Have they done something similar? (experience signal)
- How fast can they move? (velocity signal)
- Can I get on a call quickly? (accessibility signal)

**Success Outcome:** Within 2-3 exchanges, sees relevant past work, gets a clear sense of capability, and books a call via Calendly — all without filling out a form or waiting for a sales rep.

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers (Wants)

**✅ Quickly validate technical capability and relevant experience**
- WHAT: Proof that Londonberry has done similar work successfully
- WHY: Vendor selection is a risk — wrong choice wastes months
- WHEN: First 60 seconds of interaction
- **Widget Promise:** AI surfaces relevant portfolio pieces and case studies inline, matching to what the PM describes. "We built something similar for [client] — here's how that worked."

**✅ Get on a call with a real human fast**
- WHAT: Frictionless path to a calendar booking
- WHY: They're comparing vendors — the one who books first often wins
- WHEN: Once capability is confirmed (2-3 exchanges in)
- **Widget Promise:** Calendly integration surfaced at exactly the right moment. No forms, no "someone will get back to you."

**✅ See speed and quality of thinking in real-time**
- WHAT: The conversation itself demonstrates how Londonberry works
- WHY: How you communicate during sales = how you communicate during the project
- WHEN: Throughout the entire interaction
- **Widget Promise:** AI anticipates questions, offers relevant context before being asked, and demonstrates structured thinking. The conversation IS the demo.

### Negative Drivers (Fears)

**❌ Fear of wasting time on a vendor who can't execute at enterprise scale**
- WHAT: Investing time in a small shop that can't handle their requirements
- WHY: They've been burned before — great pitch, terrible delivery
- WHEN: Initial evaluation, first 60 seconds
- **Widget Answer:** AI demonstrates enterprise awareness — NDAs, compliance, integration complexity, stakeholder management — without being asked.

**❌ Fear of a generic sales pitch that doesn't understand their context**
- WHAT: Cookie-cutter responses that could apply to any project
- WHY: If the AI can't engage specifically with their problem, why would the humans be different?
- WHEN: When describing their project specifics
- **Widget Answer:** AI engages with specifics, asks targeted technical questions, and references analogous work. Not "we can do anything" but "here's how we'd approach your specific challenge."

**❌ Fear of slow response loops that delay their timeline**
- WHAT: "Fill out this form and we'll get back to you in 24-48 hours"
- WHY: They're evaluating now, decisions are being made this week
- WHEN: End of conversation, conversion moment
- **Widget Answer:** Instant engagement, real-time calendar availability, and same-day follow-up commitment.

**During:** "This is different — the AI already figured out what I need, showed me a relevant case study, and I didn't have to fill out a form. Let me book that call."

**After:** "I just booked a call with Londonberry in under 3 minutes. They clearly understand enterprise work. Moving them to top of the shortlist."

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Generate Qualified Leads (PRIMARY):** Highest revenue-per-lead persona. One enterprise engagement can be worth 10-50x a small project. Fastest path from conversation to contract.
- ✅ **Build Trust & Differentiation (SECONDARY):** The speed and intelligence of the conversation differentiates Londonberry from every other agency with a generic contact form.
- ✅ **Operational Intelligence (TERTIARY):** Enterprise conversations reveal market demand at scale — what companies are building, what tech they need, what budgets look like.
